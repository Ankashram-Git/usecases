✅ What Should Be Captured — And Where
TouchpointIdeal SystemData Captured
Stock InwardInventory app / VyaparBrand, category, size set, colour, MRP, cost price, quantity, batch date
Billing / POSRetail POS / Marg / BusyItem-wise sale, size, colour, price, customer phone, payment mode, time of day

📉 What Actually Happens
  • Stock purchased based on what the supplier salesperson pitches, not last season's sell-through data
  • Billing done item-wise but size and colour not recorded — only total amount
  • No footfall counter — owner has no idea how many people walked in vs. bought
  • Slow-moving sizes discovered only when physically checking shelves at season end
  • Discounts given inconsistently — one staff member gives 10%, another gives 20% on same item
  • Loyalty managed informally — "she's a regular" — with no purchase history backed by data
  • Dead stock donated or sold at heavy loss at season end, every year, with no learning applied
💸 What This Gap Costs You
No Size-wise Tracking
You always run out of M and L but have 40 pieces of XL unsold. Next order you repeat the same mistake because you have no data to correct it.
No Footfall Data
On a busy Saturday, 80 people walk in and only 12 buy. You think it was a good day. It was actually a 85% rejection rate — a huge signal you missed.
No Stock Ageing Alerts
A batch of kurtas sits for 5 months. You finally sell them at 50% off. If you'd flagged them at 60 days, a 15% discount would have cleared them profitably.
No Discount Control
Two customers bought the same item on the same day — one paid ₹850, one paid ₹650. Different staff, no system, inconsistent pricing erodes trust and margin.
No Customer Repeat Data
Your top 20 customers drive 40% of revenue. You don't know who they are. You spend on flyers for new customers while your loyalists quietly shift to a competitor.
No Supplier Quality Data
One supplier's fabric consistently fades after 2 washes. Customers complain. You keep ordering from them because they offer the best price — not knowing the real cost is in returns and reputation.

📉 What Actually Happens

💸 What This Gap Costs You
No Cost-Per-Meal Tracking
Owner charges ₹80 per tiffin, assuming good profit. Actual ingredient + gas + delivery cost is ₹72. She is working 10 hours a day for ₹8 per tiffin.
No Cancellation Tracking
The same 5 customers cancel every Monday. Owner cooks for them anyway. Food wasted every single week — a predictable, avoidable loss.
No Payment Tracking
₹4,000 outstanding from 6 customers. Owner feels uncomfortable asking. By month 3, she stops following up. Money is effectively lost.
No Menu Performance Data
The dish that gets the most compliments never appears in the weekday menu. Data would show it drives the most customer retention and referrals.
No Capacity Visibility
She can't confidently take on 10 new customers because she doesn't know her current maximum capacity — only by gut. Growth is blocked by lack of data, not by lack of demand.

No Drop-off Detection
A member who attended 5 days a week goes to 0. No one notices for 3 weeks. A simple "we miss you" on day 5 could have saved the membership.
No Batch Occupancy Data
The 6am batch has 18 members and feels chaotic. The 8am batch has 4. Data would let you design an incentive to balance — but you don't know the numbers.
No Referral Recognition
Your 3 most loyal members referred 11 new members between them. You have no idea. They've never been properly thanked. They're slowly feeling undervalued.

9

60–70%

✅ What Should Be Captured — And Where
TouchpointIdeal SystemData Captured
Social Media DiscoveryInstagram Insights / Meta Ads ManagerReach, profile visits, which post drove the most traffic, follower demographics
Enquiry / DMWhatsApp Business / CRM like ZohoLead name, product interest, city, how they found you, response time
Order PlacementOrder form (Google Form / Shopify / Instamojo)Product, variant, quantity, address, phone, payment method, order date
Production / InventoryProduction tracker / spreadsheetBatch size, ingredient cost, units produced, cost per unit, yield
Shipping & FulfilmentShiprocket / Delhivery dashboardDispatch date, delivery time, RTO (return-to-origin) rate, courier performance
Payment CollectionRazorpay / UPI with order taggingOrder-wise payment, prepaid vs. COD ratio, pending payments, refunds
Customer Review / UGCWhatsApp follow-up / Google FormSatisfaction rating, whether they'd reorder, photo review for social proof
Repeat PurchaseCustomer purchase history sheetOrder frequency, average order value, favourite products, last order date
Returns & ComplaintsReturn log / complaint trackerReason for return, product involved, resolution, refund issued
📉 What Actually Happens
  • Orders received via Instagram DMs and WhatsApp — tracked in the owner's head or in one chaotic group
  • No order form — address and details collected in back-and-forth chat messages, often with errors
  • Cost per unit never calculated — pricing based on "what feels right" or what a similar brand charges
  • Shipping booked manually per order — no dashboard, no RTO rate awareness
  • Payments collected via UPI but never mapped to specific orders — reconciliation done by memory
  • No follow-up after delivery — no review collected, no re-purchase nudge
  • No tracking of which Instagram post or reel drove the most enquiries or sales
  • Returns handled emotionally, case by case — patterns never identified
  • Customer database = scattered DMs across Instagram, WhatsApp, and one notebook
💸 What This Gap Costs You
No Cost-Per-Unit Tracking
You sell a jar of homemade pickle for ₹250. Ingredient + packaging + label + shipping = ₹230. You're running a business at ₹20 profit while working 6 hours per batch.
No Content Performance Data
You posted 20 reels this month. 2 of them drove 90% of your enquiries. You don't know which 2. Next month you post 20 more, hoping for the same magic.
No RTO Tracking
15% of your COD orders are being returned undelivered (RTO). Each return costs you shipping both ways + packaging. That's ₹5,000+ per month in invisible losses.
No Repeat Purchase System
You have 200 past customers. If 20% reordered just once more this month, that's 40 orders — without spending a rupee on new customer marketing. But you have no list to reach them.
No Review Collection System
Customers love your product but you have no process to capture that love. A competitor with fewer sales but more reviews wins the trust of the next buyer scrolling Instagram.
No Order-Payment Reconciliation
At month end, 8 orders have no corresponding UPI payment. Some were delayed, some never paid. You only discover this when your bank balance doesn't match your order count — weeks later.

The One Thing to Remember
Data culture doesn't mean dashboards or Excel sheets.
It means making one decision per week based on a number, not a feeling.
Start small. One question. One number. One action.
